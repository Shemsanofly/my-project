Welcome to Zanzibar

Embark on a journey to Zanzibar, an archipelago renowned for its pristine white-sand beaches, vibrant Swahili culture, and rich historical tapestry. Whether you're seeking relaxation, adventure, or cultural immersion, Zanzibar offers an unforgettable experience.

Discover the magic of Stone Town's winding alleys, spice plantations that awaken your senses, breathtaking sunset dhow cruises, and luxurious beachfont resorts. Zanzibar is not just a place, it's an experience of a lifetime, where every moment becomes a cherished memory.

Top Attractions in Zanzibar

  • Stone Town:Explore the UNESCO World Heritage site filled with winding alleys, spice markets, and centuries-old architecture.

    • It is the the historic heart of Zanzibar City, located on the west coast of Unguja Island. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique blend of Arab, Persian, India, and European influences.

    Stone Town offers a glimpse into Zanzibar's rich history, making it a must-visit for anyone interesed in culture, heritage, and architecture.



  • Nungwi & Kendwa Beaches:Swim and sunbathe on some of the world’s most breathtaking beaches.

    • Nungwi is one of Zanzibar's most popular beaches, located at the northern tip of the island. It's known for it's crystal-clear waters, white sand, and vibrant nightlife. Unlike other beaches, the tides here are minimal, so you can swim any time of the day.

    Kendwa lies just south of Nungwi and offers a more laid-back atmosphere. It's famous for a beautiful sunsets, monthly Full Moon Parties, and calm waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.



  • Jozani Forest:Meet the rare Red Colobus Monkeys in Zanzibar’s only national park.

    • It is the only national park in Zanzibar and one of the island's most treasured natural attractions.It's located in the central east region of Unguja, the main island of Zanzibar, and covrs about 50 square kilometers.

    Jozani is the famous for being home to the endangered Zanzibar Red Colobus Monkey,.These monkeys are unique for their reddish coat and social behaviour. They are not afraid of people, making them easy to spot during a walk in the forest.



  • Prison Island: Take a boat trip to see giant tortoises and learn about the island's historical past.

    • It is a historic island near Zanzibar famous for its 100-year-old giant tortoises and abandoned 19th-century prison. Enjoy snorkeling in crystal-clear waters and relaxing on white sandy beaches. Just a 30-minute boat ride from Stone Town, perfect for a half-day trip.



  • Spice Tours: Experience the island’s rich spice heritage on a guided farm tour.​

    • Zanzibar Spice Tours, immerse yourself in fragrant plantations to smell, taste, and learn about cloves, vanilla, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Enjoy fresh coconut treats and watch locals climb palm trees.
